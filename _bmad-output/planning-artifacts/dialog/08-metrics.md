## The North Star Metric: The External Pull Request

The single most important signal of success is receiving an **unsolicited technical Pull Request** from a researcher or lab member not previously known to the project within the first **6-12 months**.

**Rationale**: A PR demonstrates that the tool is integrated into a real workflow, is worth the time to improve, and that the researcher feels a sense of co-ownership. It is the ultimate proof of adoption and technical depth for recruiters at DeepMind/AMI.

---

## Success Dimensions & Milestones

### 1. User Behavior (The Adoption Loop)
- **M1-M3 (Engagement)**: 5 active PhD students/researchers asking deep technical questions in GitHub Issues (proving real-world pipeline integration).
- **M3-M6 (Validation)**: At least one paper submitted to arXiv mentioning PhysLink in the experimental or infrastructure section.
- **M6-12 (Institutionalization)**: First external PR and first lab forking the repository to extend it for a specific domain.

### 2. Intellectual Leadership (The Signal)
- **M6 (Positioning)**: Publication of a "Position Paper" on arXiv outlining the principles of World Model abstractions and the PhysLink implementation.
- **M6-M9 (Visibility)**: Presence/Mention in a specialized workshop (e.g., CoRL, RSS, or NeurIPS Embodied AI workshops).

### 3. Career Impact (The Outcome)
- **M12-M18 (Moment of Truth)**: First incoming inquiry or recruitment-related email from a target lab (DeepMind, AMI, FAIR) directly referencing the project or position paper.

---

## Critical Execution Constraint: Maintenance Responsiveness

To ensure the "Trojan Horse" strategy succeeds among time-constrained PhD students:
- **Response Time**: Maintain a <48-hour response time on all technical issues and PR reviews for the first 6 months.
- **Onboarding Quality**: The "Friday Afternoon Test" must remain a priority—zero-friction setup and a working notebook within 10 minutes.

---

## Rationale for SMART Goals
- **Specific**: Targets PhDs at specific lab tiers; focuses on a position paper on arXiv.
- **Measurable**: PR counts, issue engagement, arXiv citations, response time.
- **Achievable**: Leverages user's IaaS background for infrastructure quality.
- **Relevant**: Aligned with the career goal of joining top-tier AI labs.
- **Time-bound**: Clear milestones from Month 1 to Month 18.
