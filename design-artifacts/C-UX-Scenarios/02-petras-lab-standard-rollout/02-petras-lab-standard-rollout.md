# Scenario 02: Petra's "Lab Standard" Rollout

## Strategic Context

- **Transaction**: Formal recommendation of PhysLink as the lab's reproducibility standard.
- **Business Goal**: Objective 1 (Co-ownership) & Objective 2 (Authority).
- **Persona**: Petra (The Prolific Post-doc).
- **Situation**: Monday morning at the lab after seeing Hugo's success on Friday. Preparing arguments for the weekly meeting with Professor Paula.

## Driving Forces

- **Hope**: Building a legacy by instantiating high-quality research standards in the lab.
- **Worry**: Staking her reputation on a tool that might disappear or be judged "non-scientific."

## Interaction Model

- **Device**: Desktop (Drafting a memo/slides).
- **Starting Point**: Opens the GitHub README with a binary question: "Can I stake my reputation on this?"

## Success Definition

- **User Success**: Copies the ArXiv link and Lab Adoption Guide URL into her memo for Paula. Prof. Paula validates the choice.
- **Business Success**: PhysLink is integrated into the lab's internal README as a reference resource.

## The Path (Shortest Journey)

1. **GitHub README** — Filters for credibility (MIT License, ArXiv link, contributor count).
2. **CHANGELOG** — Verifies maintenance cadence and release regularity.
3. **ArXiv Position Paper** — Validates intellectual depth and scientific rigor. **MOMENT OF TRUTH (GO/NO-GO)**.
4. **Documentation: Lab Adoption Guide** — Confirms collaborative features (buffer sharing, AdaptationJobs).
5. **GitHub Templates (PR/Issues)** — Final signal of community maturity and sustainability.
6. **Paula's Meeting (Narrative Step)** — Formal approval based on scientific rigor and institutional independence. ✓
